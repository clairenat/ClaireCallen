Koinoike Branding, Identity, and Logo Design

Brand and Identity

Koinoike is a project I made for my graphic design class wherein I was given a project brief from a "client" that tasked me with creating a logo for the company as well as a brand and identity guide.

The "Koinoike" brand aims to educate, inform, and promote the interest of keeping koi decorative fish. The name, Koinoike, roughly translates to Koi Pond in Japanese. Raising koi fish is time-consuming and requires much more care than just sprinkling flakes into a fishbowl. Koinoike aims to build a koi-keeping network to support those who keep, raise, and appreciate the beauty of koi, and are interested in doing so in a substantial, eco-friendly way.

The client needed a logo and color swatches for their branded products. The logo will be used as the app icon, on the website, printed materials such as buisiness cards and brochures, package design, and outdoor banners. The clients wanted a logo that scales well, has modern, easy-to-read typography, and is ideally orange or red. The clients also made it clear that they did not want their logo to be two or three fishes swimming in a circle.
